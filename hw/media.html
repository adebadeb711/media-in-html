Dhaka Residential Model College

Dhaka Residential Model College was established in 1960 by the President of Pakistan, Field Marshal Ayub Khan, on an area on about 52 acres along Mirpur Road near Sher-e-Bangla Nagar, Dhaka.

In 1962, the then East Pakistan Provincial Government took over the administrative responsibility of Dhaka Residential Model College. In 1965, the government converted the institution into an autonomous institution and its administration was entrusted to a Board of Governors with the Chief Secretary as its Chairman. In 1967, the government again took control of the school. At that time, a new Board of Governors was formed with the Education Secretary as its Chairman. The school started its XI and XII classes in 1967.

The primary purpose of establishing the DRMC was to provide education for the children of the elite military officers and high-ranking government officials of East Pakistan. The school, later the college, was modeled after public schools in the United Kingdom (as per the British Public Schools Act 1868), particularly Eton College.

The school's house system was modelled on Eton. After Bangladesh's independence, a Board of Governors was appointed with the Education Secretary as its chairman. Since its establishment, the school has been financed by government grants and minimal student fees. The Board of Governors is empowered to "make such rules as may be necessary for the proper functioning of the school".

The school started with only the morning shift, which runs from 07:30 AM to 12:10 PM. In March 1993, another shift known as the day shift was added in accordance with the government's education expansion policy to cope with the increasing number of students. The day shift runs from 12:30 PM to 5:10 PM.

In this educational institution, co-curricular programs are arranged for the students to achieve physical and mental well-rounded excellence. Morning physical exercise and afternoon sports are compulsory for residential students here. Football , cricket , hockey , volleyball , basketball are played regularly. Students of this college participate in various co-curricular activities at the national and international levels. In addition to the curriculum-based programs, co-curricular programs are arranged for the students to achieve physical and mental well-rounded excellence. Morning physical exercise and afternoon sports are compulsory for residential students here. Football, cricket, hockey, volleyball, basketball are played regularly. Apart from this, there are arrangements for inter-class sports and competitions such as table tennis, carrom, chess etc. A week-long annual sports and cultural competition is held at the beginning of the year. In addition, to stimulate cultural consciousness among the students, annual weekly inter-house stage competitions are held. These include debates, quizzes, Bengali and English spelling, azan, qiraat, hamd-naat, etc. During the annual cultural week, exciting and enjoyable competitions such as speech, recitation, acting, storytelling, drama, music, painting, etc. are organized. Science fairs are organized every year to develop the science-based talent of the students and make them science-minded. Activities such as collecting postage stamps, painting, photography, gardening, clay work, music practice, cubing, scouting, drama practice, science club, debating club, band education, junior cadet corps, etc. are also held regularly. Every year, the students of each house publish an impressive wall newspaper. In addition, college annuals, science magazines and debate souvenirs are published regularly. Each task is conducted based on student participation and competition.
